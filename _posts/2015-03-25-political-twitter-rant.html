So, I went on a bit of a rant over on twitter this evening, after reading a bit about our upcoming Presidential election this weekend, and for posterity sake, I decided to post it here as well.

Enjoy.

Hi twitter!. I thought I should pop in for a few minutes and share my thoughts on the up coming elections in my country Nigeria

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


Up until this moment, I've purposefully decided to not get involved in conversations concerning the elections — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

I cannot vote as I am not in the country at the moment, and even if I was, I had decided not to vote for one basic reason

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


I have no confidence in both candidates. I definitely do not want the incumbent back, and I am weary of deciding for the other party as well — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

Its all well and good to talk about wanting change, but change can go both ways. You can have a change that makes things worse

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


When people try to convince me about why I should vote for Buhari, they mostly use the argument of wanting change. — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

So, basically, just for the fact that you want change, you have to vote for the other guy, since that's the only option you have

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


Thats a terrible argument in my opinion. You need to have something more important that your status as a challenger to be your selling point — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

Another reason people give for supporting Buhari is his Vice president. From experience,the vice president is mostly invisible to the public

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


So, why would this time be any different? He seems highly intelligent and qualified and all that, but why isn't he up for the presidency — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

I can't vote for a candidate solely based on the fact that I like his deputy. That's just not enough!

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


And then there's all the news of grandiose statements being made. "stabilizing oil prices", "stablilizing exchange rates", etc.

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

Granted, most of these could have been taken out of proportion, and I'll be the first to admit I haven't taken time out to authenticate them

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


But all this just goes to distill whatever confidence I had built up in his ability to improve our lot — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

It's not like the incumbent is any better though.

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


If there's one thing President Jonathan has proven in his time in office, its that he's terribly unfit for the position. — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

I'm not saying I could do any better. Running a country is hard, and for a country like Nigeria, probably 10x as hard

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


So, if you know in your heart of hearts that you lack the mental accume, or strong will to do the job, then why run for the position — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

So, I find myself at a quandry. Two less than desirable candidates, and no third alternative.

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015


At this point, as a voter, you might as well roll the dice and vote for whichever name comes up.It effectively amounts to the same thing IMO — Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015

That's it for my twitter political rant!. I'm going back to not having an opininon on political matters in Nigeria. Over and out!

— Muyiwa Iyowu (@muyiscoi) March 25, 2015
